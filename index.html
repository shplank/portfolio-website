about me

I grew up in Southern California and developed a passion for creation at a young age. From my dad, I learned how to use tools and work with wood, and my mom encouraged me to read. Before long I was doing carpentry projects and writing stories, and later, making music.
After high school I went to UC Santa Barbara as an English major, where my love of literature led me into what would become a long relationship with live theater. I acted, directed, wrote plays, built sets, and even helped run a small theater company in Hollywood for several years. It was tough to make a living in theater, so I decided to try something more reliable - teaching.
After years of giving my all to being an amazing high school English teacher, I ended up stressed, exhausted, and frustrated about how difficult it was to do the job well. So I left. Not long after that, the Covid-19 pandemic hit and I decided to make one more career pivot. Learning to code has been daunting, but I love this weird combination of language and building and look forward to making lots of cool stuff.
I'm a full-stack web developer based in California and trained via the CareerFoundry program.
